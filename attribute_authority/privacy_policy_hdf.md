# Attribute Authority (AA) privacy policy and description

## Description of the service
The Attribute Authority (AA) issues, verifies, and manages user attributes and attestations so identities, group membership, affiliations, and authorization policies can be handled consistently across linked services (e.g., Helmholtz Data Federation). AA serves as a central service for authentication- and authorization-related attribute management. The service is operated by the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT).

## What personal data is collected and why (purpose of data processing)
Each time you access the AA the following information is collected:
- First name, last name, email address, organization (if provided), and identifier released by your Identity Provider / OIDC Provider (IdP/OP).

This data is necessary for account management purposes (e.g., to contact you about service changes or for security) and for the reasons given below.

Log records of your access to and actions on the AA are retained. These records contain:
- Information mentioned above (personal name, email, identifier)
- The network (IP) address from which you access the AA
- Time and date of access
- Details of actions you perform (e.g., attribute requests/updates, invitations sent)

This data is necessary to ensure that the AA is reliable and secure, including assisting in the analysis of reported problems, contacting you if a problem is identified with your account, and responding to security incidents. This data may also be used for authorised services acting on behalf of authorised users.

## Who your personal data is disclosed to
The collected personal data is only accessible by the authorised personnel of KIT, and then only for reasons outlined above. Your data may be disclosed to outside parties in accordance with relevant HDF security policies and the GÉANT Code of Conduct (v2 draft), as part of incident response procedures or where required by law. Subprocessors used for hosting, storage, email delivery, and monitoring are bound by data protection terms.

## How to access, rectify, and delete your personal data
The AA receives personal data from your IdP/OP. If your provider permits editing, you may update the data there. Changing certain data (e.g., the persistent identifier sent by the provider) may affect access to some functionality.
For data retained by the AA (e.g., attribute records, invitations, audit logs), contact the service managers listed below to request access or rectification. Deletion requests will be honored where feasible and not in conflict with security, audit, or legal obligations.

## How long your personal data will be retained
Records of your use of the AA, collected for reasons of security (described above), will be deleted, at latest, 24 months after your last use of the service.
Other personal data can be deleted immediately or on request as described above.
