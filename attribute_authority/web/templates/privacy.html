Privacy Policy

Description of the Service

The Attribute Authority (AA) issues, verifies, and manages user attributes and attestations so identities, group membership, affiliations, and authorization policies can be handled consistently across linked services (e.g., Helmholtz Data Federation). AA serves as a central service for authentication‐ and authorization‐related attribute management. The service is operated by the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT).

What Personal Data Is Collected and Why

Each time you access the AA the following information is collected:

  • First name, last name, email address, organization (if provided), and identifier released by your Identity Provider / OIDC Provider (IdP/OP).

This data is necessary for account management purposes. Log records of your access contain:

  • Personal name, email, identifier
  • The network (IP) address from which you access the AA
  • Time and date of access
  • Details of actions you perform (e.g., attribute requests/updates, invitations sent)

This data is necessary to ensure that the AA is reliable and secure, including assisting in the analysis of reported problems, contacting you if a problem is identified with your account, and responding to security incidents.

Processed Data (OIDC Scopes)

The following OIDC scopes are requested from your Identity Provider:

openid profile email

Example of released claims (provider dependent):

  • name, given_name, family_name
  • email
  • preferred_username / sub (subject)
  • iss (issuer)
  • groups / affiliations (if released)

Who Your Personal Data Is Disclosed To

Personal data is not regularly disclosed to third parties. Data may be shared with authorized KIT operational staff, linked services strictly for authorization decisions, and incident response teams where required by policy or law.

Data Retention

  • Attribute records and invitation metadata are stored while active and may be retained up to 24 months after last activity.
  • Access logs are deleted after 12 months.
  • Users may request earlier removal by contacting the service.

Transfer of Personal Data Outside the EU/EEA

Personal data is not intentionally transferred outside the EU/EEA. If a sub-processed service outside the EU is required, standard contractual safeguards will be applied.

How to Access, Rectify, and Delete Your Data

To rectify data released by your Home Organisation / IdP, contact its operators. For data stored by the Attribute Authority (attributes, invitations, logs), contact the service below to request access, correction, or deletion (subject to legal/audit constraints).

Data Protection Code of Conduct

Personal data is protected according to the GÉANT Code of Conduct for Service Providers and applicable EU and German data protection law.
